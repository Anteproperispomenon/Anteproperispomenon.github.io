Apple/Pear/etc... Crisp

This is a basic recipe my mother has been using for a while. Usually, you'd just use apples or maybe pears as the base fruit, but I find it more interesting when you use a mixture of fruits.

Ingredients

Fruit Mixture Topping/Crust Mixture
5 Cups Fresh/Frozen Fruit 1 Cup Rolled Oats
2 Tbsps Whole Wheat Flour 1/4 cup Whole Wheat Flour
2 Tbsps Orange Juice or similar 1/2 cup Chopped Nuts
Cinnamon to taste 1 tsp Cinnamon
~2 Tbsps Maple Syrup or Honey 1/4 cup Brown Sugar
1/4 cup Olive Oil or Melted Butter

Directions

Preheat Oven to 400°F or 205°C. You'll need a mixing bowl for the topping/crust, a glass or aluminum baking pan (with edges at least ~4 inches/~10cm high), and (optionally) a bowl to mix the fruit mixture in.

Making the Fruit Mixture

If using apples/pears, peel them and then cut into slices. Prepare other fruit as desired. Next, add non-tender* prepared fruit, flour, orange juice, cinnamon, and maple syrup/honey to the pan/mixing bowl. Mix the fruit and other ingredients together to distribute the flour etc... This will help thicken the juices produces by the fruit as it bakes.

*Tender in this case just means fruit that would fall apart if mixed around with a metal spoon; e.g. fresh kiwi slices.

After you've finished mixing the fruit/flour/etc, transfer it to the pan (if applicable), and then add any tender fruit you hadn't added yet.

Making the Topping/Crust Mixture

Chop up the nuts to desired size. Usually, I use pecans and walnuts, but you can also use almonds. I haven't tried using hazelnuts, but I imagine they'd taste fine. If you're feeling adventurous, you can try using black walnuts (if you can get them).

Next, using the (other) mixing bowl, add the oats, flour, nuts, cinammon, and brown sugar. Drizzle the oil/melted butter on, then mix.

Baking

Sprinkle the topping mixture evenly on top of the fruit in the baking pan. If fruit mixture is uneven, just sprinkly a similar depth of topping throughout the pan.

Bake until fruit mixture is bubbling from below the crust, and the crust is golden brown. This is usually about 20~25 minutes. Remove from oven and let sit for about 10 minutes.
